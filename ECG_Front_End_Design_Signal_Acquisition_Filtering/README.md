# ECG Front-End Design

### Low-Noise Amplifier, Filtering and Signal Acquisition

## Project Overview

This project focuses on the design and implementation of an **ECG (Electrocardiogram) front-end system** capable of acquiring extremely weak bio-potential signals from the human body and converting them into a clean, observable waveform.

The system demonstrates the complete signal path from **biological signal acquisition to digital visualization**. It includes stages for signal acquisition, amplification, filtering, and real-time display using a microcontroller-based system.

Because ECG signals are extremely small and easily corrupted by noise, careful signal conditioning and filtering are required to obtain a usable waveform.

---

# 1. From Signal to Insight

The goal of the system is to capture very weak analog signals generated by the heart and convert them into a clear and stable digital waveform.

The design includes the following stages:

### Signal Acquisition

Capturing extremely small bio-potential signals produced by heart activity using biomedical electrodes.

### Signal Conditioning

Reducing noise and preparing the signal for amplification.

### Precision Amplification

Amplifying the weak differential signal while rejecting common-mode noise.

### Data Visualization

Displaying the processed ECG waveform in real time using a digital display.

# 2. Introduction to the ECG Signal

An **Electrocardiogram (ECG)** records the electrical activity generated by the heart during its contraction and relaxation cycles. It is widely used in medical diagnostics to monitor cardiac health and detect abnormalities such as arrhythmias.

### Signal Characteristics

Amplitude
ECG signals are extremely small, typically ranging between:

0.05 mV – 5 mV

Frequency Range

0.05 Hz – 100 Hz

### Main Challenge

The ECG signal is often buried under various types of noise including:

• Muscle activity (EMG)
• Power line interference
• Motion artifacts
• Baseline drift caused by breathing

Therefore, proper **signal conditioning and filtering** are essential for accurate measurement.

# 4. ECG Electrodes

Standard **Silver–Chloride biomedical electrodes** are used to measure bio-potential signals from the body.

### Function

These electrodes convert **ionic currents from the body into electrical signals** that can be processed by electronic circuits.

### Typical Electrode Placement

RA – Right Arm
LA – Left Arm
RL – Right Leg (Reference / Ground)

These placements allow measurement of the electrical potential difference generated by the heart.

# 5. Signal Acquisition

The electrodes capture extremely weak electrical signals generated by the heart muscle.

These signals must be carefully conditioned before amplification because they are easily affected by:

• Environmental noise
• Electromagnetic interference
• Movement artifacts
• Power-line hum

The signal conditioning stage prepares the signal for accurate amplification and processing.

---

# 6. Instrumentation Amplifier Stage

An **Instrumentation Amplifier** is commonly used in biomedical signal processing because it can amplify very small differential signals while rejecting noise.

### Key Features

High Common Mode Rejection Ratio (CMRR)
Allows rejection of noise that appears equally on both inputs.

Adjustable Gain
Amplifier gain can be controlled using an external resistor.

High Precision
Designed for accurate measurement of small differential voltages.

### Gain Formula

G = 1 + (49.4 kΩ / RG)

The gain is determined by the external resistor value.

---

# 7. Analog Signal Conditioning

To obtain a clean ECG waveform, multiple filters are used to remove different types of noise.

## High-Pass Filter

Cutoff Frequency: **0.5 Hz**

Purpose

Removes **baseline drift** caused by breathing or slow body movements.

---

## Notch Filter

Cutoff Frequency: **50 Hz**

Purpose

Removes **power line interference**, which is one of the most common sources of noise in biomedical signals.

---

## Low-Pass Filter

Cutoff Frequency: **100 Hz**

Purpose

Removes **high-frequency noise**, including muscle activity signals (EMG).

This ensures a clean signal for analog-to-digital conversion.

# 9. System Design Considerations

Several important challenges arise when designing an ECG acquisition system.

### Weak Bio-Potential Signals

ECG signals are extremely small and require precise amplification and filtering.

### Noise Sensitivity

External electrical interference and muscle activity can easily distort the signal.

### Safety Requirements

Biomedical circuits must ensure electrical safety when connected to the human body.

Dedicated ECG front-end modules are often used because they provide:

• Integrated amplification
• Built-in noise filtering
• High stability and safety
